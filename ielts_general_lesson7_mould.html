IELTS General Reading — Lesson 7

Workplace and Everyday Documents Under Pressure | Mould in a Public Health Building
Section 3 is an original IELTS-style passage based on the ABC article about mould problems at Casuarina Plaza in Darwin.

General Training Reading practice. This lesson emphasises everyday notices, workplace safety documents and a longer general-interest text.

Exam habit: do not use outside knowledge. If the text does not say it, choose NOT GIVEN.

Section 1 — Questions 1–14

Read the short everyday texts below and answer Questions 1–14.

Text A — Medical Centre Notice

Harbour Road Medical Centre is changing its appointment system from Monday. Standard appointments are 15 minutes. Longer appointments must be requested for paperwork, mental health plans, minor procedures or more than one health concern. Patients who arrive more than 10 minutes late may need to rebook. Masks are available at reception for patients with cold or flu symptoms.

Text B — Rental Inspection Notice

The next routine inspection for Apartment 12B will take place on Wednesday between 10:00 am and 12:00 noon. Tenants do not need to be present. Please ensure access to the balcony, laundry and smoke alarm. If you have pets, please secure them safely. Maintenance requests should be sent by email before the inspection day.

Section 2 — Questions 15–27

Read the workplace texts below and answer Questions 15–27.

Text E — Workplace Mould Response Procedure

Staff who notice mould in any workplace area must report it through the online maintenance system. Do not attempt to clean large patches of mould yourself. Small surface spots may be wiped only if gloves are worn and the area is not close to electrical equipment. Items used by clients, such as mats, toys or shared tools, must be removed from service until they have been inspected.

If mould appears on air-conditioning vents, ceiling tiles or carpets, staff must notify the site manager immediately. These areas require specialist assessment. Rooms with a strong musty smell should not be used for client appointments until management has confirmed they are safe.

Text F — Temporary Office Relocation Guidelines

When a work area is closed for cleaning or safety reasons, staff may be temporarily moved to another office. Employees should take only essential files, their laptop, charger and security pass. Confidential documents must not be left on desks in the closed area.

Client appointments may be moved to a different room, delivered online or postponed. Staff must contact clients as early as possible if an appointment location changes. If a client says they are uncomfortable attending the building, staff should offer an alternative location where available.

Section 3 — Questions 28–40

Read the longer text below and answer Questions 28–40.

A Mould Problem in a Health Building

A. A building in Darwin used by health services has been dealing with serious mould problems for several years. The site contains a community health clinic as well as offices connected with environmental health work. The Northern Territory Health Department acknowledged that the problem had been recurring and described it as unacceptable.

B. The Health Services Union said mould had significantly affected parts of the first and second floors. According to the union, staff had been regularly cleaning mould from workstations and from equipment used with clients, including therapy mats and children’s toys. Union representatives said reports about the mould were being made daily.

C. The union was especially concerned because young children attended the building for assessments and therapeutic interventions. It argued that health professionals should not be left to clean persistent mould from clinical equipment while waiting for clearer answers. The union also said the issue had been raised through appropriate channels on numerous occasions.

D. The union called for staff to be moved out of the building, but the chief executive of NT Health said relocating everyone was not necessary. She said NT Health had engaged a specialist environmental consultant to prepare a report. The purpose of the report was to identify the underlying cause of the mould and help develop a permanent solution.

E. Health advice on the NT Health website says people may be exposed to mould every day without problems. However, high levels of airborne mould spores may create health risks for people who are sensitive. Mould can also trigger asthma attacks and make respiratory or allergic conditions worse.

F. NT Health said it had already closed off some parts of the building while waiting for the report. It had also arranged specialist deep cleaning of surface mould. Other measures included targeted carpet replacement and reducing humidity by extending air-conditioner hours. Staff were also asked to reduce water sources such as pot plants, fish tanks and air fryers.

G. Officials said patients who did not feel comfortable attending the building could be offered alternative locations where possible. The report was expected to be completed in the coming weeks. Casuarina Plaza is not owned by NT Health; it is leased to the Northern Territory government.

Questions 28–32 — Matching Information

Which paragraph contains the following information? Write A–G. You may use any letter more than once.

28. Examples of equipment affected by mould.
29. A statement about alternative places for patients.
30. An explanation of possible respiratory effects.
31. Actions taken to reduce humidity.
32. Information about who owns or leases the building.

Questions 33–36 — True / False / Not Given

Write TRUE, FALSE or NOT GIVEN.

33. NT Health said the mould problem had existed only for a few weeks.
34. The union asked for all staff to be relocated from the building.
35. The specialist report had already been finalised.
36. All clients cancelled their appointments at the building.

38. What type of expert had NT Health engaged?
